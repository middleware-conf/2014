Middleware for Next Generation Internet Computing

Description

The topics of particular interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Architectures and platforms for Middleware for Next Generation Internet Computing
  • Middleware support for dependability, security, performance, and scale
  • Middleware support for adaptivity, resilience, and elasticity for novel Internet computing systems
  • Evaluation of and experience reports about middleware for novel Internet Computing systems

Information

  • Organizer: Karl M. Goeschka (Vienna University of Technology, Austria) and Miguel Matos (High Assurance Software Lab, INESC TEC and U. Minho, Portugal)
  • Contact: Karl.Goeschka@tuwien.ac.at
  • Website: http://www.dedisys.org/mw4ng14/
  • Program

Adaptive and Reflective Middleware

Description

The topics of particular interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Design and performance of adaptive and/or reflective middleware platforms
  • Techniques to improve performance and/or scalability of adaptive and reflective mechanisms;
  • Evaluation methodologies for adaptive and reflective middleware;
  • Experiences with adaptive and reflective technologies in specific domains

Information

  • Organizers: Fábio M. Costa (Federal University of Goiás, Brazil) and Anders Andersen (UiT The Arctic University of Norway)
  • Contact: arm.workshop2014@gmail.com
  • Website: http://site.uit.no/arm2014/

CrossCloud Brokers

Description

The topics of particular interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Holistic architecture of Broker as a Service
  • Efficient and open decision support system
  • Scalable multi-cloud and multi-application monitoring
  • Application (re)deployment in heterogeneous, multi-cloud environment
  • Experiences with multi-cloud architectures or approaches

Information

  • Organizers: Yehia Elkhatib (Lancaster University, UK), Stefan Walraven (iMinds-Distrinet, KU-Leuven, Belgium)
  • Contact: CrossCloudRsrch@gmail.com
  • Website: http://www.comp.lancs.ac.uk/~elkhatib/crosscloud

Middleware for Context-Aware Applications in the IoT

Description

The topics of interest of the workshop include, but are not limited to:

  • Middleware to transport and transform data from tiny sensors to context-aware applications
  • Composition and interoperability of IoT services
  • Distribution of IoT services among mobile devices, sensors and computers in the cloud
  • Middleware for context-awareness and location-awareness

Information

  • Organizers: Danny Hughes (Leuven University, Belgium), Chantal Taconet (Télécom SudParis, France), Sébastien Leriche (ENAC, France)
  • Contact: m4iot.2014@gmail.com
  • Website: http://lii-enac.fr/event/m4iot14
